## How to add a row

When you create `expN_*.py`:
1. Add the row at the top of the table (most recent first).
2. Fill **all** columns including "Learning → next". If you can't fill "Hypothesis" or "Learning → next", the experiment isn't worth running yet.
3. Keep "Key number" to one or two figures — the exact metric that decided the result.
4. After running, update Result and Key number with reality. **Do not edit the hypothesis after seeing the result** — that's how you delude yourself.

## Confirmed truths (so far)

- Tensor logic is a unifying primitive: einsum + nonlinearity covers KG inference, transitive closure, SSMs, and attention with the same shape.
- Symbolic composition is free and exact; bilinear embedding composition is near-random zero-shot.
- Generative replay (small GMM) beats weight regularization for continual learning at this scale.
- Semantic embeddings dramatically outperform random ones for relational learning (F1: 0.08 → 1.00).
- Semiring choice changes the meaning of the same computation, cleanly and without retraining.
- Adding SSM-style recurrent belief (`R̂[t+1] = obs + (1−obs)·forward(R̂[t], action)`) to a 16K-param forward model recovers object permanence under occlusion: in-occluder recall jumps 67% → 96-99% on the 8×8 world, stable across 20-step trajectories. The action-conditioned W slice IS the SSM's per-step A matrix; we don't need a separate recurrence mechanism. Memory is the active ingredient — both TL and MLP forward models benefit, and an equal/smaller-param MLP+memory matches or exceeds TL+memory under collision dynamics (exp40).
- TL's per-cell einsum prior gives **flatter accuracy degradation under distribution shift** than equal/larger MLPs, even though MLP wins absolute accuracy in every condition. Strongest TL-specific signal: under object-count transfer (train N=2, test N=3/4), TL holds 99.8% in-occluder recall while large MLPs (h=128/256) overfit to the 2-object input distribution and drop to 86-92%. Translation-equivariance is NOT free though — both TL (with absolute-position W) and MLPs collapse on occluder-position transfer. — exp41
- **TL has a sample-efficient advantage for object permanence** at low data: at n=50 trajectories, TL hits 95.5% in-occluder recall vs MLP's 91.1% (4.4pp gap, 2.5× lower variance). At n=2000 the gap inverts — MLP h=64 hits 99.9% vs TL's 97.9%. Crossover at n≈500. TL's overall P@2 stays flat at 88% regardless of data (structural ceiling); MLP scales from 90% → 93%. Classic bias-vs-capacity tradeoff. — exp42
- **TL with the right structural shape utterly destroys MLP on transitive closure**: a 3-scalar TL (`R ← σ(α·R@A + β·R + γ)` iterated K=4) at analytic init hits F1=1.000 zero-shot on the real tensor-project import graph from just n_train=20 random DAGs. A ~37k-param MLP (~12,400× more) tops out at F1=0.51 in-distribution and 0.38 on the same real graph at n_train=500. This works because the recurrence IS Boolean OR over matrix powers — the inductive bias is *exactly* the rule. Caveat: with neutral init (α=1, β=1, γ=-1), TL settles at a local minimum (F1=0.58 — still beats MLP, but not crushing). The structure is necessary; the init or some equivalent reparameterization is also needed. — exp44
- **TL's effective monoid is "thresholded sum-over-paths", broader than pure Boolean OR**: when trained for multipath2 (`≥2 distinct paths` target), the optimizer tunes α=3.6, γ=-3.8 so sigmoid saturates *only* when the path count is ≥2 — single-path cells get output ≈0.45 (below threshold), multipath cells get output ≈1.0. TL_OR F1=0.93 vs MLP F1=0.71. The expressive class is "≥k paths" for any threshold k, not just k=1. — exp45
- **TL fails decisively on tasks outside its monoid (parity / XOR / GF(2) arithmetic)**: target = `(# distinct paths) mod 2` collapses TL_OR to F1=0.26 — below the predict-all-positive baseline (~0.40 at 25% density), i.e., actively worse than chance. TL_LIN (no sigmoid) recovers to F1=0.52 — confirms sigmoid is the specific killer for XOR-shaped aggregation. MLP wins at F1=0.64. The structure-matching story holds: TL wins {OR, count-thresholds, monotone aggregation} and loses {XOR, parity, max/min, modular arithmetic}. — exp45
- **Non-monotonicity is necessary but not sufficient to break TL — combined with sparsity it is**: on `sd_geq_2` (reachable in ≥2 hops, non-monotone, ~13% density), TL_OR ties MLP at F1≈0.60. On `sd_geq_3` (≥3 hops, non-monotone, ~3% density), TL_OR collapses to F1=0.007 vs MLP's 0.18. The closure-shaped recurrence has enough expressivity to handle non-monotone + dense; sparsity removes the gradient signal needed to find the suppression operating point. — exp46
- **TL's iterative structural prior actively hurts trivial linear tasks**: predicting just A (the direct adjacency, no propagation needed) — TL_OR F1=0.13, MLP F1=0.57. TL_LIN F1=1.00 because β=2.05 drives linear passthrough — but TL_OR's forced sigmoid-after-iteration prevents this. The "structural commitment" of TL (mandatory recurrence) is useful when the task needs propagation, harmful when it doesn't. Implication: a "TL-or-not-TL" gating mechanism / depth-adaptive recurrence is the missing piece for tasks that span trivial → compositional. — exp46

## Confirmed failures / dangers

- Sigmoid + threshold=0.5 makes "no evidence" look like 50% confidence; appears in exp1, exp5, exp6, exp9.
- T-annealing alone is not enough; needs paired baseline calibration.
- Naive prediction-error curiosity creates obsession hotspots at boundaries.
- Half-correct physics priors actively hurt — they bias the model in a wrong direction. All-or-nothing for explicit rules.
- TL's per-cell einsum factorization (`W[a, x, y, p, q]`) cannot represent dynamics with cross-cell AND-conditions like collision, where the transition for cell (x₁, y₁) depends on the state at cell (x₂, y₂). On collision-world + occluder + memory, TL plateaus at ~91% P@2 while an MLP at half the params hits ~96%. The inductive bias is structurally violated when the world has multi-object interactions. — exp40
- An absolute-position W tensor is NOT translation-equivariant: it parameterizes dynamics separately for each (x, y) source location. When the occluder zone moves to a region not seen during training, TL has no learned W for that region's hidden-object propagation — the model collapses to ~20% in-occluder recall (MLPs collapse harder, ~5%, but neither generalizes). To get spatial invariance, parameterize as `W[a, dx, dy]` (relative shift). — exp41
- TL's sharp predictions are incompatible with sampling-based MPC under cumulative-goal-prob reward: when most random action sequences score 0 (mass not at goal cell), argmax over zeros is essentially random → the planner can't exploit a sharp model. MLP's fuzzier predictions provide hill-climbing signal under the same planner. So TL's sample-efficient point-prediction advantage (exp42) doesn't translate to multi-step planning success on navigation: TL hits ~10% success across all data sizes, MLP h=128 hits 53-73%. To use TL with planning, need a different planner (BFS over thresholded next-states, distance-shaped reward) or a task that doesn't require multi-step rollouts. — exp43
